“In marine environments, plastic wrap contributes to a larger plastic pollution crisis, but unlike other plastics, scientists are finding that PVC and PVDC do great jobs of picking up bacteria and metals. Those contaminated pieces of microplastic then harm the fish that mistake them for food.”

When we through away plastic wrap it can easily blow away out of a landfill and into the ocean. From the coast, the currents carry the plastic across the ocean eventually landing in one of the zones. The bobbing floating plastic is easily mistaken for Jellyfish, one of the turtle’s main sources of nutrients.

Overtime these plastics break down into tiny pieces called microplastics. These are eaten by all sorts of fish and wildlife, which are later eaten by humans. These plastics are toxic to us and the animals that ingest it.

We are slowly killing ourselves and ruining the biodiversity of our planet.
